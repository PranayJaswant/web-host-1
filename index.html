Rare animals in India

India is home to several rare and endearing animal species,ranging from the high- altitude,elusive snow leopard and reddsh-brown Red Panda to the vibrant Malabar Giant Squirrel.Other charmingly rare species include the tiny Pygmy Hog, the lion-tailed Macaque, and the distinctive Golden Langur.

Red Panda:

Often called the"fire fox,"this small,shy mammal is found in the Himalayan forests of Sikkim,West Bengal, and Arunachal Pradesh.They are endangered and known for their reddish-brown fur and bushy tail.



Malabar Giant Squirrel (Shekru):

Endemic to the Western Ghats ,this vibrant rodent features multi-colored fur(black,creame, and purple) and a long ,expressive tail.



These species are often endangered due to habitat loss and arec primarily protected in specialized national parks and sancturies across India.
